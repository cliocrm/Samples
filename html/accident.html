Data from Cars for Road Safety

Cameras for accident prevention use deep learning algorithms to detect if a driver is drowsy and warn him by playing audio clips and alerts.
Since phones have mobile internet, any detection of accidents or mishaps from the movement of the phone with the inbuilt gyroscope can be used to send notifications to the closest medical institutions and police officers.
The most important part is the data collected during the trips. Speed of the vehicle on a particular route at various points on the map, any reports of crime like a mugging or other attacks, unexpected diversions, etc. can provide extra information about a locality and the road conditions like temporary or permanent blockages, poor maintenance or damage due to natural causes like earthquakes or floods. This can be an input for manufacturing companies designing motor parts for a particular geographic region or even insurance companies to increase sales.
For example, cars with lower ground clearance are not suited for countries that have roads with potholes due to heavy rains and poor construction planning. Or people's preference for sports cars and higher torque for hilly regions where normal vehicles do not give enough mileage and lifetime of parts indicating a higher number of breakdowns and accidents.
Bringing such useful insights to the consumers directly and to companies in the automotive space can bring in more innovative and better products and prevent the loss of property or life due to accidents.
