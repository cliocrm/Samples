Understanding Cyber Terrorism

Cyber Terrorism can be a distant topic in our everyday lives since it is so less discussed. But the fact is that, every company and many individuals are "marked" by terrorist groups for cyber attacks as soon as they display the potential to make significant positive impact in the society.
Anything that is of prime importance to a nation - warfare, human capital, financial institutions, art and heritage etc. become the focus of terrorism. The reasons might sometimes seem completely strange like mere destruction of something simply because it is glorious and beautiful.
The infrastructure laid around information storage and communication is of the first order of defence against cyber attacks. Understanding the network and the operating system of each and every device that is connected in some way to the internal network, has to be understood thoroughly to prevent attacks.
It all starts from the hardware design and low-level assembly code. Same for the network and nodes that pass on the data. The devices accessing that data, and connected to the network, no matter how small and less significant, plays an important role in deciding the safety-level. Any glitch in the working of a device as small as a CCTV camera can have a devastating effect.
To fight cyber terrorism, it is good to start by undertanding what technological advances should be shared with the outside world and what should be kept secret in order to make sure, it is not used by terrorist groups. The fact is, cyber terrorists learn from the talent in the tech world, and have also started becoming innovative!
They can be brutal to kill people in the most wierd ways. Just because they are behind the computers, doesn't mean they are normal human beings. Their negativity can be strong enough to make millions of people suffer like anything.
